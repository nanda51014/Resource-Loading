01

What it does

Duration Check + Loading Backup (.xlsx)

Given vs required duration at one crew (Qty ÷ Rate/day) with variance % — flagged SHORT or LONG where outside ±tolerance (default 20%), never adjusted. Plus a full loading backup: qty → rate & source ref → crew make-up → duration → budgeted hours per trade/equipment, with live formulas.

Manpower & Equipment Histograms

Monthly stacked histograms of average daily manpower (8 trades) and plant (17 types), time-phased over each activity's given Start–Finish window at 1 crew's work content — demand peaks visible before mobilization.

P6 Import Sheet (.xls)

A TASKRSRC resource-assignment workbook in P6's native Excel import format — budgeted units per resource per activity = crew make-up × (Qty ÷ Rate/day) × hours/day: work content at one crew. Import via File → Import → Spreadsheet.

Productivity basis: the tool carries the consolidated productivity table (Consolidated_Productivity-Rev_1-02-Jul-26.xlsx) embedded. Productivity 1 governs all conflicted services, with one confirmed exception — MSE panel erection at 15 panels/day (Productivity 2). Skilled Labour counts from Productivity 2 are merged into general Labour. All rate conflicts between the two sources are recorded in the workbook's Conflict Register — no number was invented or averaged.
02

How to use it

Schedule first, in P6

Build activities, durations, logic and dates in P6 as usual, then export to Excel: Activity ID, Name, BOQ code, Activity Code, Qty, Unit, Duration, Start, Finish. Your dates and durations govern — the tool never changes them. Activities without an activity code are ignored.

Upload to the tool & set the calendar

Choose 5/6/7-day week and hours per day (default: 6-day Mon–Sat, 8 hrs — confirm per project). Set the shortfall tolerance if different from 20%.

Duration check at one crew

Required duration = Qty ÷ Rate/day at one crew, compared to your given duration (Duration column, else workdays Start–Finish). Outside ±tolerance the activity is flagged SHORT (1 crew won't fit — add crews or extend) or LONG (idle allowance — check rate or logic). Your call either way.

Review the flags

The tool never smooths silently. Duration shortfalls, unmatched activity codes, mapping gaps, missing quantities and missing dates are all listed for your call before you export.

Download the five outputs

Duration check (.xlsx), loading backup with live formulas (.xlsx), P6 TASKRSRC import (.xls), histogram data workbook (.xlsx), and the issues register (.xlsx) — file names carry the date for revision tracking.

04

Known limits — read before relying on it

  • Bridge (BG-*) and underpass (UP-*) activity codes are matched through a DRAFT mapping to Productivity 2 references — every such match is tagged "confirm" in the output, and unmapped codes (e.g. BG CBB, UP CB) are excluded with a reason, never guessed.
  • Rates come only from the two source sheets. Where a service had conflicting rates, P1 governs (confirmed 02-Jul-26), with one exception — MSE panel erection at 15 panels/day per P2. Every conflict is on record in the workbook.
  • Utility relocation (UT-*) rates are for relocation of existing services — flagged as a distinct basis from new-build utility work.
  • Duration variances (given duration outside ±tolerance of required at 1 crew) are flagged SHORT/LONG, never adjusted or smoothed silently.
  • The P6 import uses internal resource IDs (LAB, CARP, EXCVR…) — create matching resources in P6 or map on import.
